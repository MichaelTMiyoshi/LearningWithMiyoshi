# Rubric - Job Skill Presentation

A job skill presentation is just what it says it is.  Present a skill to the class.

The goal is to present a skill to the class that has not been presented before.  Or present a skill in more detail than has previously been presented.  Skills that have already been presented by the instructor or by other students may not be presented.  However, skills that have been previously presented may be expanded.  You may show more detail or more nuance to a skill previously presented.

The job skill presentation is an all or nothing proposition.  You either do it or you do not do it.  However, there are a few stipulations to doing it.  You must present a skill with enough detail to satisfy the instructor.  "How do I do that?" you ask.  I am glad you asked.  The way that you satisfy the instructor is by first making sure that you are presenting a relevant skill.  Ask first, if you are not sure your topic or skill is relevant.  Then, make sure that your presentation gives enough detail to show the instructor that you know what you are talking about.  Make sure that you can do the skill, that you know the topic.  Really know.  The way you show that you know is by answering questions to the instructor's satisfaction.  Make sure that you know the ins and outs of your skill.  Make sure that you know alternative ways to do what you are demonstrating.  Make sure that nobody can trip you up with a question.  But if you do not know, do not make things up.  Admit that you do not know it all.
